Price

Things you should know about proposed pricing (click to hide detailsclick to show details)

In this tab, Proponents are asked to propose a Total Phase Cost for the following indicated Phases.  If a maximum budget amount is identified in this tab for any Phase(s), the Total Phase Cost must not exceed that amount.  The automated system will add the sum of the Total Phase Costs and calculate the Total Proponent Cost displayed on this tab.  The Total Proponent Cost must be within the maximum budget amounts specified on this tab.

The Proponent is free to nominate a Team with as many of Resources as it believes appropriate for the Opportunity, provided that the Proponent stays within the maximum budget amounts specified in this tab.

The automated BC Developer’s Exchange system will not permit a Proponent to submit a Proposal unless this mandatory requirement is satisfied.

How Pricing Will be evaluated:

The Total Proponent Cost will be evaluated and scored according to the following formula:

Lowest Total Proponent Cost of all Shortlisted Proponents that have progressed to this stage divided by The eligible Proponent’s Total Proponent Cost multiplied by Maximum Number of Points allocated to the Pricing component.

Example: $100,000(lowest price) divided by $125,000(your price) times 10(available points for pricing) equals 8 out of 10

References

Things to know about your proposed references (click to hide detailsclick to show details)

The Province will require Proponents to support their claims of over-all experience by supplying references who can verify the quality of work provided specific to the relevant experience of the Proponent.  On the References tab, the Proponent must submit three References including company name, contact name, phone number and email address. References from the Proponent’s own organization or from named subcontractors are not acceptable for the Proponent.

The Province may in its sole discretion, but is under no obligation to, check Proponent references without first notifying the Proponent.  The Province reserves the right to seek additional references independent of those supplied by the Proponent, including internal references in relation to the Proponent’s performance under any past or current contracts with the Province or other verifications as are deemed necessary by it to verify the information contained in the Proposal and to confirm the suitability of the Proponent.

The Province may only check the lead shortlisted Proponent’s references but reserves the right to check the references of other shortlisted Proponents in the event the lead Proponent is deemed unsatisfactory.  If the Proponent is deemed unsuitable by the Province in its sole discretion due to unsatisfactory references, or if the Proposal is found to contain material errors, omissions or misrepresentations, the Proponent’s Proposal may be rejected.
