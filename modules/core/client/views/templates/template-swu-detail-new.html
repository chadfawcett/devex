Background

The technologies that support mine oversight for British Columbians need to be reimagined. This project will replace the legacy Mine Management System with a scalable, open source, data driven system using modern and flexible technologies referred to as the Mines Digital Services (the “Product”).

The Product will have a number of interconnections and relationships to systems across the Natural Resource Ministries and will be important not only to the Ministry of Energy, Mines and Petroleum Resources but also to interagency collaborations across ministries. The future state must be intuitive, and capable of providing meaningful data to relevant stakeholders.

The Product is anticipated to include:

  1. Enhancements to the public-facing BC Mine Information website (mines.nrs.gov.bc.ca) to broaden the available data and create new or improved functionality to increase usability
  2. Enhancements to the BC Mine Information website’s administrative console (mines.empr.gov.bc.ca), including work required to connect with other systems (e.g., the Natural Resource Inspection System, aka NRIS)
  3. Support for integrated analysis and decision making across the process of mine oversight
  4. Support for the creation of a complex data model that can connect various components of mines. Some examples include consultation information, risk management modelling, spatial data, financial tracking and collecting and physical attributes

We are seeking new ideas and emerging technologies to improve the applications that support mine oversight. We anticipate that throughout the inception and proof of concept phases, we will further identify scope and feature needs using a design focus and user research.
