Sprint With Us

A new procurement process for a new way of working together.

You heard right — we're learning to be agile.

The Government of B.C. is forming dedicated teams around digital services and equipping them with the modern tools needed to continuously deliver improvements. To scale this transformation, we've created Sprint With Us — a new process to quickly hire teams from the private sector. If you get hired through a Sprint With Us opportunity, here's what you can expect:
Government Product Managers

You'll work closely with a trained government product manager who has the expertise and the responsibility to run the service.

Interdisciplinary Teams

Your team will possess all the skills necessary for continuous delivery, such as DevOps engineering, front-end and back-end development, and user experience research and design.

Iteration

You'll be adaptable to change, building and validating features iteratively with users.

Phased Work

Donâ€™t expect lengthy requirements. Following an Agile Scrum process, you'll start with Inception to understand the business problem, then build a Proof of Concept to demonstrate the feasibility of the solution. Finally, you'll build out the rest of the product in Implementation.

Open Source

Your team will own the code that's produced, but it will be published in GitHub under an open source license.

Pricing & Incentives

These are not time & materials contracts! For each phase, you'll charge a fixed price for your interdisciplinary team to meet the deliverables for the time period. There's incentives to complete a phase early!

The Evaluation Process.
Less investment to get in the game. Invest more only after you have a significant chance of winning and a platform that allows your team to shine.

Applying

Submitting a proposal is a simple as telling us the team members you're putting forward, answering a series of brief questions about them, and providing a fixed price for the duration of the opportunity.

Shortlisting

To shortlist proposals, answers to the questions are scored blindly (that means the evaluators can't see the identity of the companies who submitted the answers). Dependent on the spread of resulting scores, the evaluation committee decides how many proposals to shortlist. Once they decide, the app reveals which companies made it onto the shortlist.

You'll be notified right away so you can know if your team was shortlisted or not.

Code Challenge

If your team is shortlisted, they'll complete a code challenge to demonstrate their technical capability and ability to organize as a team.

Code challenges are designed to require about 2 days of effort for a small development team. Your team will have 4 days.
